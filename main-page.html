Cardbuster

Cardbuster is a merge between action-adventure and deckbuilder genres. While keeping the strategic style of a deckbuilder, we wanted to make an action filled real-time game. In Cardbuster you use Cards which grant you abilities for a limited time to change the gameplay.

After implementing basic movement I have developed the architecture of the ability system. I have used Scriptable Objects as a way to store information about the abilities' both aesthetic and mechanical elements. Mechanical elements include damage, damageType, effectRadius, radiusAngle, abilityTarget, abilityDuration, attackSpeed etc. Aesthetic elements include name, abilityImage, vfx, sfx etc. Using a card utilizes this stored information to enable using the corresponding ability to either defeat enemies or overcome platforming challenges.

I also have made VFX such as ability effects. I have prepared sprites to use in Unity's Particle Effects and Visual Effect Graph tools working on Adobe Photoshop.

Life Socks

Life Socks is 2021 Global Game Jam entry game for our team. Life Socks is a 2D-platformer-puzzle game in which you control a sock who has lost their pair trying to find them. Player has 2 item slots in which they can carry either keys to open doors or power-ups (video 1). There are power-ups such as 'Feet' which enable you to walk faster/jump and if you have 2 'Feet' items you can double-jump (video 2), 'Umbrella' item enables you to glide long distances (video 3).

I took part in programming and level design of this game. I also did the implementation of the level in Unity3D.

Chasing After the Light

Chasing After the Light is 2022 Global Game Jam entry game for our team. Chasing After the Light is a 2D platformer game in which Player tries to stay in the light radius while the light object moves forward on its own (video 1) to keep the sanity level high. Player loses sanity if they stay out of the light (video 2). If the sanity bar drops to zero or player hits the spikes the game ends (video 3). Player gains points as long as they stay alive. Player increases their score multiplier proportional to how close they are to the light source.

I did all the programming and level design of this game using Unity3D.

Back to Hometown

In Back to Hometown, You play as a wizard who returns to their town after many years to find it corrupted by an alien force. Using your abilities you kill monsters and cleanse the area. 1st ability is a single projectile fired from your staff (video 1). 2nd ability is an area of effect damage cast in front of the Player (video 1). 3rd ability is an area of effect ability cast around the player which heals themselves and clears the corruption around (video 2). Boss corrupts an adjacent tile every few seconds and corrupted tiles allows portals to open above them to spawn more monsters (video 3).

I did programming, game design and level design of this game.

(Un)reliable Artifact

(Un)reliable Artifact is our Ludum Dare 49 entry for our team. In this game You have an unstable artifact which explodes in random intervals cleansing the corrupted buildings and road tiles in range (video 1). With every cleansed building/tile you receive points. While cleansing a building restores it, cleansing a road tile destroys the tile (video 2), so you need to be careful to not fall into the abyss through destroyed parts of the road (video 3).

I did the programming and level design while taking part in the game design process
